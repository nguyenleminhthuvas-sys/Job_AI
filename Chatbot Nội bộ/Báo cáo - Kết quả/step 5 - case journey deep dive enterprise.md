## PHẦN 4: PATTERN RECOGNITION

> **Labeling standard:** Patterns are labeled STRUCTURAL INFERENCE unless backed by cross-case primary evidence. They represent consultant-level synthesis, not proven causal claims.

---

### Pattern #1: "Measure Outcome, Not Activity" — INFERENCE (supported by Klarna verification)

**Verified anchor:** Klarna's failure is demonstrably linked to measuring resolution speed rather than customer satisfaction. This is confirmed by the strategic reversal.

**Pattern claim:** Successful cases tracked multiple outcome metrics including accuracy, satisfaction, and time saved simultaneously. This is documented at pattern level but NOT confirmed case-by-case with primary data.

**Lesson:** Define success metrics BEFORE deployment; include customer/user satisfaction alongside operational efficiency.

---

### Pattern #2: "Escalation = Moat" — VERIFIED (Klarna failure case) / INFERENCE (success cases)

**Verified anchor:** Klarna explicitly lacked an escalation path; this is confirmed by both the chatbot scope and the strategic reversal.

**Inference:** JPMorgan, Morgan Stanley, and IBM all appear to have human review steps built in — based on trade press descriptions, not primary architecture documentation.

**Lesson:** Build escalation path on Day 1. Complex cases require human judgment; AI handles high-volume routine cases.

---

### Pattern #3: "Change Management = Value Multiplier" — INFERENCE

**Claim:** 20–30% of implementation effort should be allocated to change management.

**Evidence strength:** This figure appears in The Missing Link blog (Tier 5). It aligns with general enterprise transformation literature but is NOT verified as a specific threshold by any primary source in this document set.

**Label: INFERENCE — treat as directional guidance, not a validated benchmark.**

---

### Pattern #4: "3–6 Month Pilot Saves 6–12 Month Disaster" — INFERENCE

**Verified anchor:** Klarna appeared to rush to full deployment without adequate pilot duration — inferred from the rapid rollout timeline and subsequent reversal.

**Pattern claim:** JPMorgan and Morgan Stanley conducted controlled pilots before scaling. Documentation of exact pilot timelines is NOT available from primary sources.
